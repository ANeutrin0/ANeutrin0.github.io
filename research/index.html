Zhu Ji

Undergraduate in Computational Communication. Research Intern at Microsoft Research Asia.

Research

My research focuses on computational systems that help people search, evaluate, synthesize, and reason over complex information. I work across social computing, computational communication, HCI, and LLM agent systems.

Current Research Themes

LLM Agents and Workflow Design. I study and build LLM-agent workflows for task decomposition, tool orchestration, retrieval-augmented reasoning, evidence synthesis, and report generation.

LLM-as-a-Judge and Evaluation. I am interested in how large language models can support complex evaluation tasks, especially when judgments require evidence aggregation, comparison, and qualitative reasoning.

Social Computing and Scholarly Search. My current work includes AI-supported academic talent discovery, research trajectory analysis, representative work summarization, and research-fit assessment.

Selected Projects

Copanion. An AI agent framework developed in the Social Computing Group at Microsoft Research Asia. I contribute to reusable agent workflows for search, reading, verification, synthesis, and structured reporting.

TalentScope. An academic talent discovery system built on Copanion. I lead system design and implementation for candidate identification, publication analysis, research trajectory summarization, and research-fit assessment.

FAIR. A study of how large language models are changing competitive programming. My contributions include large-scale Codeforces data collection and analysis, behavior-based evidence analysis for hidden AI use, and a semi-structured interview with an ICPC World Finals champion.
